Idiom Confusion/Combined

I get a lot of idioms wrong, combined or just mis-aligned to meet my own needs from its purpose or creation. Most common ones include:


The horse has left the cart somewhere = 'The cart has left the horse.' Wait, I cannot even tell if this is right anymore. What is the saying?


The bag has left the cat = 'The cat is out of the bag.' <-- but what I'm really saying is cover your eyes, its going to get ugly... when I use this one.


Coal in the Cannery mind = I later discovered this was "Cannary in the coal mine." And then still don't use it right


Read herring = I thought this was all 'signal' look at me! Apparently its a distractor! All these years, I often wondered why people didn't take my point serious when I used this saying. I now know why.


Play it by year = "Play it by ear"
